Aconitum is also referred to as Wolf's bane or Wolfsbane. Their nickname stems from the fact that most types of Aconitum are extremely poisonous. They grow from perennial plants in the Northern Hemisphere and there are over 250 species.

Alyssum plants are an excellent plant for absolutely any garden. They typically grow 3 to 6 inches tall and are dieal for flower beds and walkways. Though they're a self sow plant, their weakness is frost. Consider your climate before planting them and deciding whether or not this flower is for you.

Anthurium

Other Name:

Flamingo Flower

Season:

All Seasons

Sun:

Partial Shade

Soil:

Well-Drained

Zones:

10 - 12

More:

If indoors or in a steady climate, the anthurium plant can bloom all year. If you're new to plant care, the anthurium plant may not be for you though. They're a bit tricky to care for and can be difficult to get to bloom. The lighting you pick for your anthurium plants is very important. With too little or too much sun, they can begin to turn brown or stop blooming. If you're inexperienced in caring for flower, but really want an anthurium plant, choose a potted variety. Potted anthurium plants can be easily moved so that you can test and choose the best area for it to grow.

Asclepias Syriaca

Other Name:

Purple Milkweed

Season:

Summer

Sun:

Full

Soil:

Moist Loamy

Zones:

3 - 9

More:

Sometimes called stick weed or caterpillar food, this perennial plant features balls of colored flowers. There attract bugs and insects of all kinds from monarch caterpillars, honeybees, and hummingbird moths. While plantign them can be useful to help attract animals needed in your garden, you want to plant them in a secluded area. Plant them away from your main garden so that the underground rhizomes don't attack your other plants.
